# Fiverr Portfolio Review: "Cyber Security Homelab"

This is a great, detailed write-up of your lab environment! However, much like your first gig, this is written for a hiring manager reviewing your resume rather than a *client looking to buy a service*. Clients want to see how your skills solve their problems.

Here are the key areas to adjust to make this portfolio item a client magnet:

## 1. The Description (The "About" Text)

* **Current Issue:** Phrases like "A self-directed research and development project..." and "...applying the principles of the Google Cybersecurity Professional Certificate" sound like you are a student practicing, not an expert offering professional services.
* **The Fix:** Frame the homelab as a "Simulated Enterprise Environment" that you built to test advanced detection strategies.
* **Action:** Change the intro to something like: *“Designed and deployed a fully functional, multi-OS enterprise network simulation to engineer advanced Splunk SIEM detection rules and document SOC Incident Response procedures.”*

## 2. The Visuals (Screenshots)

* **Current Issue:** The screenshots show technical interfaces (Nano text editor, Windows Network Properties, an empty Splunk login screen). While they prove you built the lab, they don't visually communicate *value* to a client. Clients don't want to buy network configurations; they want to buy security.
* **The Fix:** Show the *results* of your work, not just the setup.
* **Action:** Replace these images with screenshots of your actual **findings**. Take screenshots of:
  * A Splunk Dashboard showing a brute force attack visualization.
  * A snippet of your `SOC-001.md` incident report.
  * A snippet of your BloodHound attack path map.

## 3. The Strategy: Break It Up

* **Current Issue:** You have crammed your SIEM, Linux Hardening, and Vulnerability Assessment into one single portfolio project. This dilutes your message.
* **The Fix:** As we discussed earlier, you should split this into the **Three Targeted Projects** we created the PDFs for.
* **Action:** Keep this "Homelab" piece if you want, but you MUST add the 3 specific projects from your `Gig_Attachments` folder:
    1. **Enterprise SIEM Deployment & Detection** (Upload the Splunk PDF)
    2. **Web Application Vulnerability Assessment** (Upload the Pentest PDF)
    3. **SOC Incident Response Playbooks** (Upload the IR PDF)
