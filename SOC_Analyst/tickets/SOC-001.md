---

## Summary

Multiple failed SMB login attempts from 192.168.50.30 followed by a successful authentication for `j.smith`. Pattern consistent with a password spray attack using CrackMapExec.

---

## Detection

- **Alert:** Brute Force — Multiple Failed Logins
- **Source:** Splunk (EventCode 4625 / 4624)
- **Trigger:** 10 failed logins from a single IP within 2 minutes

---

## Investigation

### Step 1: Confirm the Alert

```spl
index=main EventCode=4625 Source_Network_Address="192.168.50.30"
| stats count by Account_Name | sort -count
```

**Result:** 5 accounts targeted — j.smith, j.doe, b.wilson, svc_sql, s.bostick (2 failures each)

### Step 2: Check for Success

```spl
index=main EventCode=4624 Source_Network_Address="192.168.50.30"
| table _time, Account_Name, Logon_Type
```

**Result:** `j.smith` — successful SMB login at 10:16 AM. **Account compromised.**

### Step 3: Check for Post-Compromise Activity

```spl
index=main Account_Name="j.smith" earliest=-1h
| stats count by EventCode, source | sort -count
```

**Result:** j.smith was used for Kerberoasting (EventCode 4769) at 12:10 PM — see SOC-002.
