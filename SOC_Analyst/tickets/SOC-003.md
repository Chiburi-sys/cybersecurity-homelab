## Summary

A high-volume port scan was detected from 192.168.50.30 targeting 192.168.50.20 (Windows 11). 1000 ports were probed in under 60 seconds. After investigation, this was confirmed as an authorized penetration test.

---

## Detection

- **Alert:** Port Scan — High Volume Connection Attempts
- **Source:** Splunk (Sysmon EventCode 3 — Network Connection)
- **Trigger:** >100 unique destination ports from a single IP within 5 minutes

---

## Investigation

### Step 1: Quantify the Scan

```spl
index=main src_ip="192.168.50.30" dest_ip="192.168.50.20"
| stats dc(dest_port) as unique_ports, count as total_connections
```

**Result:** 1000 unique ports scanned, 1000 total connection attempts.

### Step 2: Identify Open Ports

```spl
index=main src_ip="192.168.50.30" dest_ip="192.168.50.20" action="allowed"
| stats count by dest_port | sort -count
```

**Result:** Ports 135, 139, 445 responded as open (SMB/RPC services).

### Step 3: Verify Authorization

- Checked with change management — **authorized pentest activity**
- Source IP 192.168.50.30 is the designated Kali Linux attack machine
- Scan was part of the scheduled vulnerability assessment
